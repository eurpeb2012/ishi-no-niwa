## 1. Cultural Context {#cultural-context}

### Power Stones (パワーストーン)

In Japan, healing gemstones are commonly called **power stones** (パワーストーン). The practice sits within a broader spiritual movement called **Seishin Sekai** (精神世界 - "Spiritual World"), which parallels Western New Age traditions but is deeply rooted in Shinto animism and Buddhist philosophy.

### Historical Timeline

| Period | Era | Significance |
|--------|-----|--------------|
| Jomon | ~5,500 BCE | First jadeite carvings in Itoigawa; magatama beads made from clay, talc, quartz, jade |
| Kofun | 300-538 CE | Magatama become ceremonial/religious objects; symbols of power and authority |
| Nara | 710-794 | Chrysanthemum imported for medicinal properties; stone symbolism deepens |
| Heian | 794-1185 | Jade, quartz, agate used as protective charms (omamori); widespread spiritual belief |
| Modern | 2000s | "Power stone boom" - mainstream popularity of crystal bracelets and accessories |
| 2016 | -- | Jadeite selected as Japan's National Stone (国石) by JAMS |
| 2021 | -- | Japanese industry associations add 10 new regional birthstones |

### Shinto Stone Traditions

- **Nade Ishi** (なで石) - Stones rubbed for healing at shrines
- **Kaname Ishi** (要石) - Stones believed to stabilize the balance of the world
- **Yorishiro** (依代) - Objects (including stones) that attract kami spirits
- **Shintai** (神体) - Sacred bodies where kami reside (stones, mirrors, swords)
- **Kōsekigaku** (鉱石学) - Traditional Japanese crystal healing practices
- **Scrying** - Using crystal quartz spheres to see the future; quartz spheres = "heart of a dragon"

## 2. Magatama - The Original Power Stone {#magatama}

### Overview

**Magatama** (勾玉) are curved, comma-shaped beads - Japan's most iconic spiritual stone form.

- **Name**: maga (曲 = curved) + tama (玉 = precious stone/gem)
- **Shape**: Comma/embryo-shaped with a hole for stringing
- **Period**: Final Jomon (~1000 BCE) through Kofun period (6th century CE)
- **Materials**: Originally clay, talc, slate, quartz, gneiss; later almost exclusively jade

### Spiritual Properties

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Sun & Moon Energy | Shape represents sun and moon; worn to receive celestial blessings |
| Ancestral Connection | Strung on cord to connect wearer with ancestors' spiritual power |
| Vitality | Generates vibrations that resonate with human energy, strengthening life force |
| Protection | Repels disaster and misfortune; shields physical health |
| Mental Health | Brings confidence, good fortune, and emotional stability |
| Kami Vessel | Functions as yorishiro (attracts kami spirits) in Shinto rituals |

### Imperial Significance

The **Yasakani no Magatama** is one of the **Three Imperial Regalia of Japan** (三種の神器), alongside the mirror (Yata no Kagami) and sword (Kusanagi no Tsurugi).

## 3. Japan-Native Gemstones {#japan-native-gemstones}

### Itoigawa Jade (糸魚川翡翠)

- **Location**: Itoigawa and Omi regions, Niigata Prefecture
- **Status**: Japan's National Stone (国石) since 2016
- **History**: Birthplace of jadeite carving (~5,500 years ago)
- **Color Varieties**:
  - White - nearly pure jadeite
  - Green - Fe-rich, Cr-bearing
  - Lavender - Ti-bearing
  - Blue - Ti- and Fe-bearing
  - Black - graphite-bearing
- **Healing Properties**: Serenity, luck, anxiety reduction, wisdom, mercy, stable peace
- **Japanese Name**: Hisui (翡翠)

### Sugilite (スギライト)

- **Discovery**: First found in Japan in 1944 by petrologist Ken-ichi Sugi
- **Other Sources**: South Africa, Canada, India
- **Color**: Deep purple/violet
- **Chakras**: Crown and Third Eye
- **Healing Properties**:
  - Emotional: Calming, stress/anxiety relief, emotional release
  - Physical: Supports central nervous system, neuralgia, immune recovery
  - Spiritual: Enhances psychic abilities, spiritual awareness, life purpose discovery
  - Meditation: Facilitates connection to higher consciousness

### Chrysanthemum Stone - Kiku Ishi (菊石)

- **Discovery**: Neodani, a mountain valley in central Japan
- **Composition**: Grey limestone with andalusite, celestite, feldspar, calcite (petals) and chert (stamen)
- **Appearance**: Natural chrysanthemum flower pattern embedded in stone
- **Cultural Meaning**: Chrysanthemum (kiku) = longevity, renewal, resilience, imperial authority
- **Healing Properties**:
  - Helps see true life purpose
  - Assists in recognizing opportunities
  - Focus on the present moment
  - Improves sleep, prevents nightmares

### Japanese Amethyst (日本産アメジスト)

- **Location**: Itoigawa/Omi regions, Niigata Prefecture
- **Characteristics**: Known for deep, rich color
- **Japanese Name**: Murasaki Suisho (紫水晶)
- **Healing Properties**: Calms mind, reduces stress, promotes restful sleep, dispels negativity
- **Historical**: Purple symbolizes spirituality, intuition, transformation

### Japanese Quartz / Suisho (水晶)

- **Name Meaning**: Sui (水 = water) + Sho (晶 = crystal) - "water crystal"
- **Location**: Yamanashi Prefecture (high transparency)
- **Reputation**: "Ice of heaven"
- **Healing Properties**: Spiritual purification, mental stability, clarity, wisdom
- **Traditional Use**: Scrying (crystal ball divination), dragon heart symbolism

### Japanese Agate (瑪瑙 / メノウ)

- **Location**: Found throughout Japan
- **Connection**: Deep ties to Buddhism
- **Characteristics**: Beautiful layered colors; a type of chalcedony
- **Healing Properties**: Balances energies, awakens talents

### Amber (琥珀 / コハク)

- **Status**: One of Japan's traditionally loved natural stones
- **Healing Properties**: Warmth, protection, vitality

- name_en: English name
- name_jp: Japanese name (katakana or kanji)
- reading: hiragana reading
- color_primary: primary color
- color_hex: representative hex color
- origin_japan: boolean - is it native to Japan?
- origin_region: Japanese region (if native)
- chakras: array of associated chakras
- intentions: array of use-case categories
- hardness: Mohs hardness scale
- rarity: common | uncommon | rare
- tier: popularity tier in Japan (1-3)
- description_short: one-line description

## Appendix A: Magatama Shape Stones

For app purposes - magatama-shaped stones are a distinct product category in Japan. Any gemstone can be carved into magatama shape, which amplifies its spiritual properties according to tradition. Most popular magatama materials:

1. Jade (traditional, most authentic)
2. Amethyst
3. Clear Quartz
4. Rose Quartz
5. Obsidian
6. Agate
